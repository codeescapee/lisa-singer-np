Common ADHD Symptoms

Inattention Symptoms

  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Easily distracted
  • Forgetfulness in daily activities
  • Difficulty organizing tasks
  • Avoiding tasks requiring sustained mental effort
  • Losing important items
  • Not following through on instructions

Hyperactivity/Impulsivity

  • Fidgeting or squirming
  • Difficulty staying seated
  • Excessive talking
  • Interrupting others
  • Difficulty waiting turn
  • Acting without thinking
  • Restlessness

Frequently Asked Questions About ADHD

What is ADHD?

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning. It affects both children and adults and can impact academic, professional, and personal life.

What are the symptoms of ADHD in adults?

Adult ADHD symptoms include difficulty focusing, disorganization, time management challenges, forgetfulness, restlessness, impulsivity, mood swings, difficulty completing tasks, and trouble maintaining relationships. These symptoms must be present for at least 6 months and significantly impact daily life.

How is ADHD diagnosed?

ADHD diagnosis involves a comprehensive evaluation including clinical interview, review of symptoms and history, standardized rating scales, assessment of functional impairment, and ruling out other conditions. At Singer Psych, we conduct thorough evaluations to ensure accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment planning.

What ADHD treatment options are available?

ADHD treatment typically includes medication management (stimulants like Adderall, Vyvanse, or non-stimulants like Strattera), behavioral therapy, lifestyle modifications, organizational skills training, and ongoing monitoring. We create personalized treatment plans based on individual needs and response to treatment.

Can ADHD be treated without medication?

While medication is often the most effective treatment for ADHD, non-medication approaches can be helpful and may include behavioral therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), coaching, lifestyle changes, exercise, sleep optimization, and organizational strategies. Many patients benefit from a combination of medication and behavioral interventions.

Is ADHD medication safe?

ADHD medications are generally safe when prescribed and monitored by a qualified healthcare provider. We carefully assess each patient's medical history, monitor for side effects, and adjust treatment as needed. Regular follow-up appointments ensure medication effectiveness and safety.

How long does an ADHD evaluation take?

A comprehensive ADHD evaluation typically takes 60-90 minutes for the initial appointment. This includes clinical interview, symptom assessment, review of developmental history, and discussion of treatment options. Follow-up appointments are scheduled to review results and begin treatment.

Do you treat both children and adults with ADHD?

Yes, Singer Psych provides ADHD evaluation and treatment for both children and adults. We understand that ADHD presents differently across the lifespan and tailor our approach to each patient's developmental stage and individual needs.
